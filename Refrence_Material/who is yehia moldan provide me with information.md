**Yehia Moldan is a Palestinian-Swedish graphic designer, lettering artist, and Arabic typographer known for his innovative fusion of language, culture, and visual design.**[^1][^2]

## Background

Born in 1990 in Gaza, Palestine, he grew up there before moving to Europe, where Arabic typography became central to his identity and work. He studied linguistics before training as a graphic designer, focusing on logos that balance functionality and aesthetics. Now based between Sweden, Berlin, and other locations, his art draws from Arabic calligraphy, philosophical poetry, and diverse influences like Bedouin tattoos and Viking symbols.[^2][^3][^4][^5]

## Career and Style

Moldan specializes in brand identity, visual systems, and Arabic typography, creating a unique style that transforms Arabic letters, words, and phrases into geometric, abstract designs popular in tattoos—over 2,000 bodies feature his work. His tattoos have appeared on celebrities like Palestinian musician Saint Levant and Egyptian actor Mohamed Hatem. Freelance projects highlight his modern take on calligraphy, inspired by classical poets and thinkers like Ibn Khaldun.[^6][^3][^7][^8]

## Online Presence

He shares his portfolio on Instagram (@yehiamoldan) and Behance, showcasing typography and designs. Interviews and features in outlets like UNION Magazine and Arabic-for-Nerds emphasize his role in elevating Arabic visual art.[^2][^9][^6][^3]
